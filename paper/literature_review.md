# Literature Review

Previous studies have extensively examined the relationship between rainfall variability and agricultural productivity.

Deotrephy et al. studied rainfall variability in Meghalaya and found a strong impact on rice yield using regression models. Bharath and Abhishek analysed Indian agriculture and highlighted the negative impact of drought and flood cycles on productivity.

Mendelsohn et al. (2001) concluded that Indian agriculture remains highly vulnerable to climate variability despite technological improvements. Mall et al. (2006) emphasized that stable and well-distributed monsoon rainfall is critical for consistent crop yields.

However, most previous studies are region-specific or focused on a single crop. This study addresses this gap by analysing national-level data across multiple crops (rice and wheat) over a long-term period (1980–2020).
