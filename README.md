# Impact of Monsoon Variability on Agricultural Productivity in India (1980–2020)

## Overview
This project analyses the relationship between monsoon rainfall variability and agricultural productivity in India over the period 1980–2020 using statistical and correlation-based methods.

The study focuses on rice and wheat yield responses to long-term rainfall fluctuations.

---

## Objectives
- To analyse monsoon rainfall variability (1980–2020)
- To compute rainfall anomalies and variability indicators
- To examine rice and wheat yield trends
- To study correlation between rainfall and agricultural productivity

---

## Data Sources
- India Meteorological Department (IMD)
- FAOSTAT (Food and Agriculture Organization)
- Open Government Data Platform India

---

## Methods Used
- Rainfall anomaly analysis
- Standard deviation & coefficient of variation
- Pearson correlation analysis
- Time-series trend analysis
- Data visualization

---

## Key Findings
- Strong rainfall variability observed across decades
- Rice yield shows higher sensitivity to rainfall fluctuations
- Wheat yield is moderately affected due to irrigation support
- Positive correlation between rainfall and crop productivity
